AI Forecasting Models Assist

We are introducing 4 AI models to assist you in making a climate-resilient forecasting decision.

TFT (Temporal Fusion Transformer) SOTA Deep Learning

Advanced multi-horizon neural network designed for high-precision time series forecasting.

Pros: Best accuracy (<2% error rate); captures complex seasonal trends during disasters.
Cons: Computationally expensive; requires high-end GPUs for training.
LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) Recurrent Network

A class of RNN designed to model sequence history and temporal dependencies.

Pros: Excellent at tracking historical sequence context and local patterns.
Cons: Slow sequence training; struggles with extremely long dependency ranges.
XGBoost (eXtreme Gradient Boosting) Machine Learning

Highly optimized tree-boosting algorithm used widely for tabular data forecasting.

Pros: Very lightweight; executes quickly on standard CPUs; moderate accuracy.
Cons: Requires extensive tabular feature engineering; moderate error margin (~12.6%).
Chronos (Chronos-2-Base) Time Series Foundation Model

Next-generation language transformer adapted for forecasting numerical patterns.

Pros: Zero-shot generalization; works out-of-the-box on new datasets without training.
Cons: Slower inference speed; higher computational overhead than XGBoost.
